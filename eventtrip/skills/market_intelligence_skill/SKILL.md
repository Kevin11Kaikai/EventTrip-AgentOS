# Market Intelligence Skill

Use this skill to infer whether event-ticket pricing reflects real demand or reseller-controlled scarcity.

## Core Principle

Do not let secondary ticket sellers force premature buying if travel-demand proxies remain weak.

## Signals

- High ticket price plus high listings plus high hotel availability may indicate reseller pressure.
- High ticket price plus low hotel availability plus high flight prices may indicate real demand.
- Falling prices plus rising listings may indicate inventory unloading.
- Stable hotel and flight prices may justify waiting.
- High social buzz can signal genuine demand, but it should be checked against travel behavior.

## Process

1. Compare ticket listings and lowest price.
2. Check whether prices are rising, flat, or falling.
3. Compare hotel availability near the venue.
4. Compare flight price pressure for each traveler origin.
5. Add social buzz as a demand amplifier, not a final answer.
6. Estimate whether the market is real-demand driven, reseller-pressure driven, or mixed.

## Output Format

- Market diagnosis
- Buy, wait, or monitor recommendation
- Scalper Stress Index summary
- Signals to re-check
- Trigger price or trigger condition
